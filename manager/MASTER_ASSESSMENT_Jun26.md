# PART 1 — The manager's question, answered

**The question:** is there a race to restock crude reserves — *if any, if even possible, and if even needed?*

### Is there a race? — **No active bidding war as of late June 2026.**
Three of the four would-be buyers are not firing:
- **China** — the supposed swing buyer is **drawing/absent**: crude imports collapsed to an 8-yr low (~6.4–7.8 mb/d); it is using its ~1.3–1.4 bn bbl buffer as a price-*suppressing* lever, buying only if Brent ≤ ~$70. [V: import data]
- **US SPR** — the "refill" is mostly **accounting, not market demand**: ~200 mb is contractual in-kind exchange-returns (Nov-26→Sep-28); discretionary cash buys are **paused** (DOE bid $50–60 WTI vs ~$69–70 spot). [V]
- **Commercial/industry (~500 mb)** — was **blocked by backwardation** (negative carry); the curve only flipped to shallow contango ~24 Jun, so refill *begins* H2-2026, into a weakening market. [V: curve]
- **EU/IEA (186 mb) + Japan/Korea (76 mb) + India (63 mb)** — all explicitly **deferred to 2027** / dip-buying; no active tenders. [V]

### Is it even possible (at scale, fast)? — **No; it is physically gated.**
- **OPEC spare (>4 mb/d) is trapped behind Hormuz** — adding spare behind a blockade yields zero exportable barrels. [V]
- **Bypass is maxed** — Yanbu ~4 mb/d (port-gated, not pipe-gated), Fujairah ~1.6; **no new capacity until 2027**. [V: Fortune/Kpler]
- **US SPR intake is capped ~3 mb/month** vs the ~4.4 mb/d drawdown — a 5–6× asymmetry; full refill is a **2028–2031** job. [V: EIA pace]
- **Grade mismatch** — drawn strategic/Gulf barrels are medium/sour; the Atlantic surge is light/sweet, so buyers partly chase *different* barrels. [EST]

### Is it even needed? — **Largely no, at the margin.**
- **Net restocking demand is small either way** (~+0.3 mb/d in the deep-dive; negative-to-negligible in the legacy briefing) against a forecast **3–5 mb/d glut**. [EST]
- **Demand destruction offsets most of the draw**: IEA 2Q26 deliveries −5 mb/d y/y (~472 mb). Whatever portion is *permanent* never needs re-buying. [V: rate]
- **Legal 90-day obligations exist** (EU/Japan/Korea) but are being deferred or met by *easing* private-stockholding mandates (Korea did exactly this in May). [V]

**One-line answer for the manager:** *There is no meaningful restocking race right now, it could not happen fast even if willed (spare and bypass are gated), and it is mostly not needed because demand destruction has cancelled most of the refill need. The decisive variable is not who refills first — it is whether the lost demand is permanent (bearish) or snaps back (bullish), and whether the Hormuz truce holds.*

# PART 2 — The dynamics: where it has been, where it is now

A four-act arc since 28 Feb (all figures source-verified unless tagged EST):

**Act I — The shock (late Feb–Mar).** Iran halts Hormuz transit (~20 mb/d total flow → ~2.7 mb/d avg Mar–May [V: IEA]). Brent spikes from ~$72 (27 Feb) toward a ~$120 peak [V/EST]. 11 Mar: IEA authorises the largest-ever **400 mb** release (US share 172) [V].

**Act II — Producer paralysis (Mar–Apr).** Spare capacity can't ship: Gulf tankage saturates in ~2 weeks → output **shut in at the wellhead**, not stockpiled — Iraq ~3.4, Saudi ~2.0, Kuwait ~1.9, Iran ~1.5 mb/d [V where country-specific]. This is the core finding: **the closure DRAINED global reserves rather than relocating them** (the ρ test, Part 5).

**Act III — The strategic drawdown (Apr–May).** Consumer/strategic stocks fall hard. US SPR **415→331 mb** (lowest since 1983, ~46% full) [V: EIA WCSSTUS1]. **OECD government inventories −163 mb to their lowest since Dec 1990** [V: IEA]. EU emergency stocks, by contrast, hold flat at the **~90-day legal floor** (Germany 91, France 92, Italy 92, Spain 92, NL 90, Poland 90) because falling net imports offset the draw [V: Eurostat].

**Act IV — The deflation (Jun, now).** US-Iran navigation deal (18 Jun); first tankers re-transit Hormuz (19 Jun); ~35 mb of stranded crude exits the Gulf; Brent falls to **~$72 (26 Jun), lowest since 27 Feb**; the curve flips to **contango (~24 Jun)** [V]. Crude flow recovering toward ~75% of prewar (~4.8 mb/d confirmed) [V: Kpler/Reuters].

**Net direction:** the scarcity premium has **fully bled out** even though strategic reserves sit at multi-decade lows — precisely *because* there is no restocking overhang and demand is soft. Reserves are **not yet rebuilding** (every published SPR print falls through 19 Jun; the 26 Jun week is not yet released).

# PART 3 — Forward outlook: two scenarios

*Scenarios are forward PROJECTIONS (EST); price anchors map to the named forecaster bench.*

### Scenario A — Truce / reopening **holds** (base case, ~60–65% subjective)
- **Reserves:** draw decelerates then stops H2-2026; **refill is slow, back-loaded and gated** (bypass to 2027; SPR ~3 mb/month → pre-crisis ~414 mb by ~2028, nameplate ~2031). No sharp rebuild.
- **Restock race:** stays a **2027 latent floor**, not a 2026 auction; commercial restock trickles in on contango; China dip-buys sub-$70; it is **self-cancelling** (rallies kill the buying).
- **Price:** **~$64–80** — JPMorgan ~$64 (2027), Goldman ~$75, Morgan Stanley ~$80; current ~$72 sits inside this band [V: bench]. Bias **lower** if demand destruction proves permanent and OPEC+ spare returns to a soft market (Rystad >3 mb/d glut end-26, ~5 mb/d 2027).
- **Timeline:** war premium gone; consumer relief lags the crude fall by the ~4-month CPI pass-through.

### Scenario B — Truce / reopening **fails / Strait re-closes** (~35–40%)
- **Reserves:** draw **re-accelerates**; Gulf re-strands; OECD govt stocks blow through the Dec-1990 low. Hard clock: US SPR → ~150 mb floor ~Nov-26 at the acute rate; an OECD-level emergency-action threshold ~mid-Nov [EST extrapolation].
- **Restock race:** irrelevant — survival sourcing dominates; refill **indefinitely deferred**.
- **Price:** **~$105–130+** — Rapidan ~$130 summer peak; RBC "risk underpriced"; a hard closure could re-rate above $120 [V: bench]. Demand destruction deepens (Rapidan: recession risk rivalling 2008).
- **Timeline:** premium returns within **weeks** of any re-closure; the ~4-month bypass/refill constraints mean **no quick relief valve**.

**The hinge between A and B is binary, not a midpoint** — averaging the two produces a number no institution holds. Watch the two switches: (1) does the navigation deal convert to a settlement, and (2) does the demand snap back or stay destroyed.

# PART 4 — Reconciling note: DEEP_SYNTHESIS vs the legacy BRIEFING

Both reach the **same practical verdict — no restocking race that matters** — but report the *net* number with **opposite signs**, and the manager should understand why:

| | Legacy BRIEFING | DEEP_SYNTHESIS (now authoritative) |
|---|---|---|
| Net restock | **−166 to +48 mb** (cumulative) | **+0.02 to +0.42 mb/d** (rate; mid ~+0.27) |
| Gross refill base | 306 mb (executed releases 186 + stranded 120) | ~900 mb cumulative deficit (commercial 500 + govt 400) |
| Demand-destruction treatment | nets the **full** 472 mb (treats most as permanent) | nets only the **permanent** slice (~0.4–0.8 mb/d), treating most 2Q26 −5 mb/d as **cyclical** |

**The single substantive disagreement** is the **permanent-vs-cyclical demand split** — and it is the very uncertainty both flag as binding. If demand destruction is mostly permanent (briefing's assumption) → net restock is negative. If mostly cyclical and snaps back (deep-dive's assumption) → net restock is slightly positive. Either way the magnitude is trivial vs the 3–5 mb/d glut, so **the conclusion is robust to the disagreement**; only the sign moves. *Recommendation: present the range, not a point, and tie it explicitly to the permanent/cyclical question.*

# Bottom line for the manager

1. **No restocking race worth pricing.** It isn't happening (China selling, US paused, commercial only just unblocked), couldn't happen fast (spare + bypass gated), and is mostly unneeded (demand destruction cancels the refill). Don't procure as if a barrel war is imminent.
2. **Reserves drained, didn't relocate** (ρ≈0.20) — and are **not yet rebuilding**, but the premium is already gone. Plan for a slow, grade-constrained, 2027+ refill — and use sub-$70 dips to lock term cover before the latent floor activates.
3. **One variable rules the outlook:** does the Hormuz truce hold *and* does lost demand snap back? That binary — not the refill schedule — sets whether 2026–27 is a **$64–80 glut** or a **$105–130 re-closure spike**.
