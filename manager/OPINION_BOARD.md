## The one-line story
The debate has **flipped from "how high" to "how durable is the reopening, and how violent is the restock."** Brent has round-tripped to ~$72, but the forecasts split into **two camps ~$40–90 apart** on the *same* benchmark — and the single thing they're all really betting on is **whether the Hormuz reopening holds.**

## Mapped to your manager's three questions
**1 · Price** — bimodal, not a midpoint. **Deal holds → $64–80** (JPM, Citi, GS, MS). **Deal fails / Iran keeps control → $86–130+** (RBC, Rystad, Energy Aspects, Rapidan). Averaging them gives a number *no one holds*.

**2 · Hormuz duration** — the load-bearing split. Bears: near-resolution, barrels already flowing, ~normal by late-July. Bulls: Iran retains *operational control*, flows cap at **60–70%**, embedding a **$10–20 permanent premium**. Everything else hinges on this.

**3 · Reserve restock** — agreement on the *hole* (OECD inventories weakest since ~2003; SPR drawn toward its ~150 mb operational floor; IEA releases tapped out), disagreement on the *refill*: bulls (McNally, Sen, Rystad) see restock demand soaking up returning barrels and holding prices up 6–18 months; bears (Kaneva, Citi) see returning Gulf barrels + trapped crude **flooding into a 2H-26 glut**.

## The fault line (what to tell your manager)
> The ~$40–90 forecast spread is **not** about OPEC+ spare capacity or China demand — it is **one bet: does the Hormuz reopening hold?** If it does, the bears are right and oil drifts to the $60s–70s on the inventory overhang. If Iran keeps a hand on the tap, the bulls are right and a depleted world pays a structural premium. That single variable is what your reserve/restock view should be conditioned on.

## What would move each camp (triggers to watch)
- **Toward bears:** Hormuz transits back >90% of prewar; AIS-on tanker normalisation; OPEC+ accelerates unwind; China demand stays weak.
- **Toward bulls:** any re-closure / mining incident; insurance/war-risk premium re-spikes; SPR hits its operational floor; ceasefire/MoU breakdown.
