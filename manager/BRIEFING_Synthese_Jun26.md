# Crude Reserves & Restocking — Synthèse Jun-2026

*Energy-markets briefing · as of 26 Jun 2026 · 118 days into the Iran–Hormuz conflict (war start 28 Feb).*

---

## Bottom line
The Hormuz closure **drained global reserves rather than relocating them.** Oil-buyers drew down their strategic/commercial stocks (US SPR at a 1983 low); producers could **not** stockpile the blocked oil (Gulf tanks filled in ~2 weeks → wells shut in), so the world's *total* reserves genuinely fell. Brent has round-tripped to ≈ pre-war (~$74), but **reserves have not started rebuilding** and won't materially until the Strait normalises — earliest Q4-2026.

## ⚠️ Two things to flag before Monday
1. **Lead with *coverage*, not barrels.** The decision-relevant metric is **days / months of net-import cover**, not million barrels. A country can hold large volumes yet thin coverage, and vice-versa.
2. **Hormuz status is verified, not assumed** — and it's the swing factor. As of 26 Jun: **contested-but-flowing, ~4.8 mb/d vs ~15 prewar** (legally declared closed by Iran ~20 Jun, physically reduced-but-open under a fragile ceasefire). Every restocking conclusion is conditional on this.

## 3 · Restocking assessment
**a) How depleted, ~4 months in?** Severely on the **consumer/strategic side** — US SPR −19% (1983 low); OECD government stocks at their lowest since 1990. The **producer side did not absorb it**: Gulf storage saturated within ~2 weeks, so ~0.7–1.1 bn bbl of output was **shut in at the wellhead**, not stockpiled. Net global reserves fell genuinely.

**b) Restock from where?** **Atlantic Basin** crude (+~3.5 mb/d rerouted East-of-Suez: US, Brazil, Canada, Kazakhstan, Venezuela) plus **bypass pipelines** (Saudi Yanbu ~4 mb/d, UAE Fujairah ~1.5). **Not OPEC spare capacity** — ~3.5–4 mb/d of it sits *behind* the Strait and cannot ship while it's constrained.

**c) At what price?** Brent ~$74 ≈ eve-of-war (~$72); the war premium has bled out. Forecasts are **bimodal, not a midpoint**: *deal holds* → ~$70–80 (Goldman ~$75, JPMorgan ~$64, Morgan Stanley ~$80, EIA ~$79); *deal fails / Strait re-closes* → ~$105–130 (Rapidan-style). The signal is the split, not the average.

**d) Timeline?** Slow and back-loaded. Bypass routes are already maxed (no new capacity until 2027); with the usual **~4-month price-transmission lag**, a meaningful refill is an **H2-2026-into-2027** event — and only if the ceasefire holds. A re-closure resets the clock within weeks.

## Data basis & honest caveat
- **Metric:** days/months of net-import cover (per the brief — coverage, not barrels). Net exporters (US, Netherlands) carry "–" because the import-cover metric is undefined for them; the **US SPR is tracked separately in §2**.
- **"Today" figures are estimates (`EST.`)** — expected and accepted under the brief. They are **agency/wire-attributed**, assembled from IEA/EIA/OPEC commentary and reputable press, **not pulled from the primary portals** (which were unreachable at build time). Treat exact figures as directional; **re-pull IEA OMR / EIA before any number is quoted externally.**
- **Confidence basis** is shown per cell (V verified / W wire / E modelled-estimate). Official and estimate are never blended in one cell.
- **Robust conclusions** (drained-not-redistributed; Europe down / Asia held; OPEC-spare trapped; refill is throughput-limited, H2-26/27): these hold regardless of decimal precision. The precise per-country days do not.
